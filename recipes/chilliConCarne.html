This great chilli recipe has to be one of the best dishes to serve to friends for a casual get-together. An easy sharing favourite that uses up storecupboard ingredients.

  • 1 large onion
  • 1 red pepper
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 heaped tsp hot chilli powder (or 1 level tbsp if using mild)
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 500g lean minced beef
  • 1 beef stock cube
  • 400g can chopped tomatoes
  • ½ tsp dried marjoram
  • 1 tsp sugar (or a thumbnail-sized piece of dark chocolate instead)
  • 2 tbsp tomato purée
  • 410g can red kidney beans
  • Plain boiled long grain rice (to serve)
  • Soured cream (to serve)

  1. Prepare your vegetables:
    • Chop 1 large onion into small dice (about 5mm square). Slice lengthways into matchsticks, then dice.
    • Cut 1 red pepper in half, remove the stalk, wash away the seeds, and chop.
    • Peel and finely chop 2 garlic cloves.
  2. Start cooking:
    • Heat a pan over medium heat.
    • Add 1 tbsp oil and heat for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Cook the onion:
    • Add the onion to the pan and cook, stirring frequently, for about 5 minutes until soft, squidgy, and slightly translucent.
  4. Add spices and vegetables:
    • Add the garlic, red pepper, 1 heaped tsp hot chilli powder (or 1 level tbsp mild), 1 tsp paprika, and 1 tsp ground cumin.
    • Stir well and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Brown the minced beef:
    • Turn up the heat, add 500g lean minced beef, and break it up with a spoon or spatula.
    • Cook for at least 5 minutes, stirring frequently, until browned and no pink bits remain.
  6. Make the sauce:
    • Crumble 1 beef stock cube into 300ml hot water and pour into the pan.
    • Add a 400g can of chopped tomatoes, ½ tsp dried marjoram, 1 tsp sugar, salt, and pepper.
    • Squirt in 2 tbsp tomato purée and stir well.
  7. Simmer gently:
    • Bring to a boil, stir, and cover the pan with a lid.
    • Reduce the heat to a gentle simmer and cook for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    • Add a couple of tablespoons of water if the sauce starts to dry out.
  8. Add kidney beans:
    • Drain and rinse a 410g can of red kidney beans and stir them into the pot.
    • Bring back to the boil, then simmer gently without the lid for another 10 minutes. Add water if the mixture looks too dry.
  9. Taste and season:
    • Taste the chilli and adjust seasoning as needed.
  10. Let it rest:
    • Replace the lid, turn off the heat, and leave the chilli to stand for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to mingle.
  11. Serve:
    • Serve with soured cream and plain boiled long grain rice.
